How to fix a water-damaged phone

A calm, step-by-step approach for the first hour after your phone gets wet.

Remove it from the water immediately

Every extra second submerged increases the chance of internal damage. Don't wait to finish what you were doing.

Power off if it isn't already

If the screen is off, don't turn it on to check. If it's on and responsive, this is a good time to run a water eject tool before powering down.

Dry the exterior thoroughly

Use a lint-free cloth on all visible surfaces, ports, and buttons. Avoid shaking the phone, which can push water deeper inside.

Avoid rice, use silica gel instead

Rice dust can enter ports and cause more harm. Silica gel packets or a dedicated drying pouch work better if you have them.

Wait at least 24 hours before charging

Charging a still-wet phone risks a short circuit. Let it air dry fully in a room-temperature, low-humidity space.

Test the speaker once it's dry

If sound is muffled or crackly after drying, run the speaker cleaner tool a few times before assuming permanent damage.

If your phone still doesn't power on after 24-48 hours of drying, it's time to see a repair technician rather than continuing to wait.
